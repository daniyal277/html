Realme

History of Realme

Realme was founded in 2018 as a sub-brand of Oppo, focusing on affordable yet high-performance smartphones. It quickly gained popularity in markets like India and Southeast Asia by offering feature-packed devices at competitive prices. In 2019, Realme became an independent brand under BBK Electronics, the same parent company as Oppo, Vivo, and OnePlus. The company expanded its product lineup to include smartwatches, earbuds, and laptops. With a strong online sales strategy and aggressive pricing, Realme became one of the fastest-growing smartphone brands, competing directly with Xiaomi and Samsung in budget and mid-range segments. Today, it continues to innovate with 5G technology and AI-driven features.

Production of Realme

Realme's production process is efficient and cost-effective, focusing on mass manufacturing while maintaining quality. The company sources components like processors, displays, and batteries from major suppliers, including MediaTek and Qualcomm. Assembly takes place in factories primarily located in China, India, and other key markets, ensuring faster production and local distribution. Realme follows strict quality control measures, including durability and performance testing, to maintain reliability. Once assembled, devices are packaged and shipped through a global logistics network. By leveraging advanced manufacturing techniques and supply chain partnerships, Realme delivers feature-rich smartphones at competitive prices.
