Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

1876 - 1948

Founder Of Pakistan

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah is primarily known as the founder and first Governor-General of Pakistan. He is widely revered as the "Father of the Nation" in Pakistan. His leadership of the All-India Muslim League and his relentless pursuit of an independent Muslim state led to the creation of Pakistan in 1947.

Biographies

  • Founder of Pakistan: Jinnah's tireless efforts and strategic leadership were instrumental in the formation of Pakistan, a separate nation for Muslims in the Indian subcontinent.
  • Leader of the All-India Muslim League: He guided the Muslim League from 1913 until the creation of Pakistan, advocating for Muslim rights and interests within British India
  • First Governor-General of Pakistan: After the partition, Jinnah served as the first Governor-General of Pakistan, leading the newly independent nation in its early years.
  • Visionary Leader: He was a skilled lawyer, politician, and statesman with a keen understanding of constitutional matters and a strong vision for an independent Pakistan.
  • Ambassador of Hindu-Muslim Unity: Jinnah was known for his efforts to promote unity between Hindus and Muslims in India.
